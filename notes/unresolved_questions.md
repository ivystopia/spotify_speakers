# Unresolved Questions

- `JBL Authentics 200`: official JBL material looks promising, but I did not find a valid exact Spotify device page for the exact model. Manual re-check is recommended before dismissing it permanently.
- `Audio Pro A10 MkII`: Spotify's exact page is for `Audio Pro A10`, not clearly the current MkII retail model. That exact-model mismatch remains unresolved.
- `Denon Home 150`: current UK pricing and stock looked retailer-led and variant-sensitive. Double-check whether a listing is for `Home 150` or `Home 150 NV`, because Spotify's exact page is for `Denon Home 150` generically rather than explicitly for the NV variant.
- `Bluesound PULSE M`: official UK pages showed current pricing and store markup, but Bluesound pages embed mixed inventory fragments. Verify stock at checkout.
- `Sound quality`: no in-person listening was done. The shortlist assumes these speakers are upgrades over Nest Audio based on category, price tier, and published consensus rather than direct side-by-side listening.
- `Android validation`: for any Chromecast-capable candidate, a real test in the target home remains strongly recommended before keeping the speaker.

## Manual Validation Still Recommended

1. Re-open Spotify's exact page for the chosen model on the purchase day and confirm the lossless tag still matches the report.
2. Test the speaker from the Android Spotify app with your usual Google Home devices still powered on.
3. If Spotify only shows a Cast target, power down Google Home / Google Cast devices and test again.
4. If the speaker only behaves correctly after removing Cast devices, treat that as a real risk rather than a setup fluke.
