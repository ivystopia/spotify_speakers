# Final Audit Notes

Date: 2026-04-14

## Result

The final ranking **stayed the same**:

1. Sonos Era 100
2. Bluesound PULSE M
3. B&O Beosound Emerge
4. Denon Home 150

I did **not** find a contradiction in the saved evidence that justified changing the order.

## What I changed

- Rewrote the report into a shorter buyer-facing version with only the genuinely relevant shortlist entries.
- Made the short answer shorter and more decisive.
- Re-structured each shortlisted model into three separate evidence buckets:
  - what Spotify confirms
  - what manufacturer docs confirm
  - what community evidence suggests
- Rewrote the Android section in plain English and tied it more directly to the saved risk log.
- Added a tighter pre-purchase checklist.
- Reduced the excluded-model section to only the two near-miss leads that still matter most.

## Claims I softened

- Softened the Sonos wording from effectively stating "Sonos does not support Chromecast" to a narrower claim:
  - the reviewed community evidence says Sonos is not a Chromecast target
  - the low-risk conclusion for Era 100 is still an inference, not a direct device-specific Android test
- Softened Bluesound wording so it no longer reads like direct PULSE M proof of Android safety. It now clearly says the case is indirect but favorable.
- Softened Denon wording from "likely avoids the Google Cast trap" to "looks less exposed" because there is no direct Denon-specific Android evidence in the saved files.
- Removed broader Android-risk wording that pointed to extra brand examples beyond what the saved source files clearly supported.
- Kept sound-quality language cautious. The report now says these speakers are plausible upgrades by class and price tier, not proven winners from direct listening.

## Remaining uncertainty

- **Sonos Era 100:** Spotify proof is strong, but the Android-risk judgment is still partly inferred from the absence of reviewed Google Cast support rather than a direct Era 100 Android case report.
- **Bluesound PULSE M:** same issue as Sonos, plus live stock status on Bluesound's UK pages should still be checked at checkout.
- **B&O Beosound Emerge:** the Android warning is the strongest in the set, but still comes from community evidence rather than official B&O or Spotify product documentation.
- **Denon Home 150:** retailer naming between `Home 150` and `Home 150 NV` remains a visible caveat.
- **Sound quality:** no direct listening or in-home comparison was done.

## Final view

The ranking still reflects the strongest balance of:

- exact Spotify-model confirmation
- compact standalone form factor
- UK availability and price
- Android Cast-vs-Connect risk

The biggest reason the order did not change is that the main tradeoffs are still clean:

- **Era 100** is the safest all-rounder.
- **PULSE M** is the stronger step-up if avoiding Cast risk still matters.
- **Emerge** is attractive but clearly riskier on Android.
- **Home 150** fails the primary 24-bit requirement.
