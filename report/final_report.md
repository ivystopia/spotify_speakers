# Compact UK Spotify Connect Speaker Shortlist

Date: 2026-04-14

## Short answer

Buy **Sonos Era 100** unless you have a specific reason to spend more on **Bluesound PULSE M**. **B&O Beosound Emerge** is attractive and Spotify-confirmed at 24-bit, but Android risk is materially higher. **Denon Home 150** is a fallback only because Spotify's exact page confirms **16-bit**, not 24-bit.

## What this ranking is based on

This ranking still holds on the saved evidence:

1. **Sonos Era 100** as the safest default
2. **Bluesound PULSE M** as the premium non-Cast alternative
3. **B&O Beosound Emerge** as the attractive but riskier Android option
4. **Denon Home 150** as fallback only because Spotify confirms 16-bit, not 24-bit

Confidence is **high** on Spotify capability, because Spotify's exact model pages were treated as decisive. Confidence is lower on Android behavior, because that part depends on a mix of official support details and community reports rather than direct hands-on testing with these exact speakers in one home.

One important caveat up front: this report does **not** prove a sound-quality ranking from direct listening. These models are plausible upgrades over a Nest Audio by product class and price tier, but not because I did an in-room comparison.

## Shortlist

### Sonos Era 100

Best default choice.

- **What Spotify confirms:** The exact Spotify page for **Sonos Era 100** lists **Lossless 24-bit**.
- **What manufacturer docs confirm:** Sonos UK showed **£199**, in stock, with **Bluetooth** and **Wi-Fi**. The dimensions are about **182.5 x 120 x 130.5 mm**, which is close to the intended tabletop size. Sonos also has official Google Assistant control material.
- **What community evidence suggests:** The reviewed Android problem mostly affects speakers that expose both **Google Cast** and **Spotify Connect**. The Sonos community material reviewed says Sonos supports Spotify Connect and AirPlay, not Chromecast. That makes Era 100 look like a lower-risk option for this specific Android problem.
- **Caveats:** The low Android-risk conclusion is still an inference, not a direct Era 100 Android test inside the target home. The Sonos Chromecast point in this report is supported by the reviewed community evidence, not by a formal Sonos spec line saying "no Chromecast."
- **Confidence:** High on Spotify confirmation, compact fit, and price. Medium on Android safety.
- **Verdict:** **Strong fit**.

### Bluesound PULSE M

Best pricier non-Cast alternative.

- **What Spotify confirms:** The exact Spotify page for **PULSE M** lists **Lossless 24-bit**.
- **What manufacturer docs confirm:** Bluesound UK showed **£429**. Official Bluesound pages list **Spotify Connect**, **Bluetooth 5.0**, **aptX HD**, **Wi-Fi**, and a one-box tabletop speaker about **171 x 204 x 149 mm**.
- **What community evidence suggests:** Bluesound community posts complain that Chromecast support is still missing. A Spotify Community user also contrasted older non-Cast Bluesound gear with newer Cast-capable gear and said the Bluesound did **not** have the same Android problem. That points toward lower risk here.
- **Caveats:** This Android conclusion is indirect. It is stronger than a blind guess, but weaker than a model-specific user report. The speaker is also larger and more expensive than Era 100, and Bluesound's UK store pages had enough mixed inventory markup that stock should be re-checked at checkout.
- **Confidence:** High on Spotify confirmation. Medium on Android safety and live availability.
- **Verdict:** **Strong fit** if you want a step up and want to minimize Cast-related risk.

### B&O Beosound Emerge

Most attractive compact premium option, but the riskiest shortlisted choice on Android.

- **What Spotify confirms:** The exact Spotify page for **B&O Beosound Emerge** lists **Lossless 24-bit**.
- **What manufacturer docs confirm:** B&O UK showed **£900**, **InStock**, **Bluetooth 5.0**, and explicit **Google Cast** support. It is a true compact standalone speaker with a very slim footprint.
- **What community evidence suggests:** This is the only shortlisted model with direct model-family warning signs in the saved evidence. A Spotify Community user reported that **Beosound Emerge** and **Beosound A9 5th Gen** showed both Connect and Chromecast on Windows, but only Chromecast in the Android app.
- **Caveats:** This does not prove every Emerge owner will have the problem. It does show that the risk is not hypothetical. If minimizing Android surprises is the main goal, Emerge is the weakest fit of the three 24-bit picks.
- **Confidence:** High on Spotify confirmation and official feature set. Medium-high that Android risk is real, but still setup-dependent.
- **Verdict:** **Promising but risky**.

### Denon Home 150

Useful fallback, not a first-choice answer.

- **What Spotify confirms:** The exact Spotify page for **Denon Home 150** lists **Lossless 16-bit**, not 24-bit.
- **What manufacturer docs confirm:** Official Denon materials describe a compact HEOS speaker with **Bluetooth**, **Wi-Fi**, **AirPlay 2**, and Alexa-related ecosystem support. Retail pricing in the saved notes was roughly **£179-£219**, depending on variant and retailer.
- **What community evidence suggests:** There is no direct Denon-specific Android evidence in the saved files. Because no Google Cast support was found in the reviewed materials, this model looks less exposed to the Cast-vs-Connect problem than Chromecast-capable rivals, but that conclusion is weaker than for Sonos or Bluesound.
- **Caveats:** It misses the main requirement that matters most here: Spotify does **not** confirm 24-bit. There is also still some naming ambiguity between **Home 150** and **Home 150 NV** in retailer listings.
- **Confidence:** High that it is a fallback rather than a primary recommendation. Low-medium on exact Android behavior.
- **Verdict:** **Fallback**.

## Why the Android risk matters

In plain English, the issue is this:

- A speaker can support **native Spotify Connect**.
- But if that same speaker, or other speakers in the home, also expose **Google Cast**, the Android Spotify app may choose to show the Cast path instead.
- When that happens, the speaker may appear in Spotify as a Cast destination rather than the native Spotify Connect endpoint you actually wanted to use.

The saved evidence supports three practical conclusions:

1. The Android risk looks **real**, not theoretical.
2. The risk looks **highest** on speakers that support both **Google Cast** and **Spotify Connect**.
3. Speakers where the reviewed evidence points to **no Google Cast support** look **safer**, but not guaranteed safe.

That is why **B&O Beosound Emerge** sits below **Sonos Era 100** and **Bluesound PULSE M** even though all three are Spotify-confirmed at 24-bit.

- **Audio Pro A10 MkII** stays out because the saved Spotify page is for **Audio Pro A10**, not clearly the current MkII retail model, and it does not confirm 24-bit.

Those two leads are still the most plausible near-misses, but they are not strong enough to displace the current top four on the evidence saved in this workspace.

## Practical pre-purchase checklist

1. On the day you buy, open Spotify's exact page for the exact model and confirm the lossless tag still matches this report.
2. Buy from a UK retailer with an easy return window.
3. In the Android Spotify app, check whether the speaker appears as a **Spotify Connect** endpoint on your normal home network.
4. If you have Google Home or other Cast devices, test again with those devices powered off or off the network.
5. If the Connect option only appears after removing Cast devices, treat that as a real warning sign.
6. If you choose **Denon Home 150**, do so only if **16-bit** Spotify support is acceptable.

## Bottom line

The ranking stays the same.

If you want the safest answer from the current evidence, choose **Sonos Era 100**. If you want a pricier non-Cast alternative, choose **Bluesound PULSE M**. If you are tempted by **B&O Beosound Emerge**, treat its Android behavior as a real risk to be tested before you keep it. Keep **Denon Home 150** in reserve only if the exact 24-bit Spotify requirement softens.
